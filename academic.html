Graduate studio work

Sustainable design explored across three scales during my Master's in Sustainable Design at Thomas Jefferson University: a city-wide public realm masterplan, an adaptive-reuse building, and a small campus installation.

01Revitalizing the Lake — The City Scale

A community-centric cultural space transforming an urban lakefront in Vadodara, India. The studio brief asked us to explore adaptability and resilience in urban contexts, using systems thinking to identify opportunities aligned with local culture and community values. The masterplan strengthens civic life across twin lakes with food plazas, an amphitheatre, riverfront seating, an open event space, and authorized waterfront kiosks.

02Train Loading Dock Reuse — The Building Scale

An adaptive-reuse project in Manayunk, Philadelphia, transforming an existing train loading dock (serving as a pop-up garden) into a durable, adaptable structure designed for a lifespan of several hundred years. The scheme leans heavily on passive design: cross ventilation, stack effect, thermal mass, light wells, and rain and terrace gardens, housing pottery and painting workshops, a cafe, exhibition space, and music rooms across two floors.

03Winter Bus Shelter — The Campus Scale

A semi-outdoor shelter for the corner of Henry Avenue and School House Lane on the PhilaU campus: a high-traffic pedestrian route and bus stop beside a congested four-lane road. The small-scale public structure balances protection from winter weather with comfort and openness for the students and faculty who cross here daily.
