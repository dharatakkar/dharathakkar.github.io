A Luxury Penthouse Design

Where cool grey tones meet warm, natural light. The spiral staircase takes centre stage as a sculptural statement in this calm and contemporary space. Across a double-height living volume, the palette moves between honed grey stone, soft textiles, and sheer full-height drapery that filters daylight into the heart of the home, while warmer zones — a fluted-walnut entrance foyer and a wood-panelled family lounge — balance the cool restraint with texture, colour, and a sense of welcome. Every element, from the curved glass balustrade to the marble-top coffee tables and suspended ring chandeliers, was selected to keep the space quiet, cohesive, and effortlessly luxurious.

01The Entrance Foyer

The arrival experience sets a warmer counterpoint to the penthouse's cool interior. Fluted walnut panelling wraps the walls and ceiling, framing an arched mirror above a curved, ribbed console in teal and timber. A circular pooja niche, backed in a floral block-print fabric and lit from within, becomes the quiet focal point of the entry, while a sculpted blue lounge chair and a hand-painted ceramic planter add colour against the honed stone floor.

02The Family Lounge

A more intimate living zone layered in colour and texture: soft blue lime-plaster walls carry a trio of framed miniature artworks, while walnut slat panelling returns as a datum that ties the lounge back to the foyer. A brass mid-century chandelier and a curved, floating display ledge in timber give the room its character, with a greige sectional, patterned rug, and a sapphire accent armchair completing the composition.

03The Double-Height Living Room

The heart of the home is a double-height volume washed in natural light. Sheer floor-to-ceiling drapery softens the glazing, and a cascade of suspended ring chandeliers draws the eye upward through the void. A generous grey modular sectional with integrated timber ledges anchors the room, paired with nesting marble-top coffee tables and a dark open shelving wall. Descending the stair, the same room resolves into a single composed view from above — the moment where the project's two ideas, calm grey restraint and warm natural light, are read together.

04The Sculptural Spiral Staircase

The staircase is treated as a piece of sculpture rather than circulation. Honed grey stone treads sweep through a helical curve, held by a frameless curved-glass balustrade with a dark leather-wrapped handrail. Its smooth charcoal underside reads as a suspended ribbon within the white envelope, while a brass inlay on the adjoining door and a velvet bench in deep teal add quiet moments of richness at its base.
